For this recipe, I use a sourdough starter (see my guide on [how to make your own sourdough starter](/posts/2026-02-09_sourdough_starter/)) and a dough base with 72% hydration. You can calculate the exact amount of ingredients for any size loaf using my [Sourdough Hydration Calculator](/projects/hydration/). One rule of thumb to keep in mind is that the hard ingredients (raisins, cranberries, and walnuts) should not exceed 25% of the weight of the flour used for your recipe to ensure the gluten structure can support them.

I personally like to use the following recipe. The pictures in this post are from a bread based on it.



---



## Ingredients List
* **Active Sourdough Starter (100% hydration):** $100.0\,\text{g}$ 
* **Bread Flour:** $525\,\text{g}$
* **Water:** $364\,\text{ml}$
* **Salt:** $12\,\text{g}$
* **Dried Cranberries:** $40\,\text{g}$
* **Raisins:** $40\,\text{g}$
* **Walnuts:** $40\,\text{g}$

## Step-by-Step Preparation

### 1. Prep the Raisins and Cranberries (30 mins before mixing)

* **The Berries:** Place the **dried cranberries** and **raisins** in a bowl and cover them with warm water. Let them soak for **30 minutes**. After 30 minutes, drain them thoroughly and pat dry with a paper towel before adding.
* **The Walnuts:** Chop the **walnuts** coarsely and then roast them in a dry frying pan on the stovetop or in the oven at $350^\circ\text{F}$ ($175^\circ\text{C}$) for about 5–8 minutes until fragrant.

---

### 2. Mix and Autolyse
* Dissolve your active **sourdough starter** in the **water** in a large bowl.
* Add the **bread flour** and **salt**.
* Mix with your hands until no dry flour remains. Cover the bowl and let it rest for **30 to 45 minutes** to kickstart gluten development.

### 3. Stretch and Fold (Integrating the Inclusions)
Instead of kneading, we build dough strength through a series of **4 stretch and folds** spaced 30 minutes apart. We will add our nuts and berries during the **second round** to allow the gluten structure to partially form first.

* **1st Stretch & Fold:** Once 30 to 45 minutes have passed since the mixing of flour, water, and salt, we can start the first stretch. Wet your hands. Lift one side of the dough up, stretch it gently, and fold it over the center. Rotate the bowl 90 degrees and repeat. Do this until you feel resistance from the dough. This means the gluten protein chains have aligned and tightened, developing structural tension. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
* **2nd Stretch & Fold — *Add Inclusions*:** Wet your hands. Gently stretch the dough out slightly in the bowl. Scatter about half of your cooled **walnuts**, soaked **cranberries**, and **raisins** over the surface. Perform your first fold. Scatter the remaining inclusions on the newly exposed surface and complete the remaining folds. This laminates the inclusions into the dough layers beautifully. Do the stretch and fold until you feel the resistance of the gluten again. 
* **3rd Stretch & Fold:** Perform another stretch and fold. The inclusions will start to be fully incorporated and enveloped by the strong gluten network.
* **4th Stretch & Fold — *Final Shaping*:** For the fourth and final stretch and fold, we transition straight into shaping. Stretch the dough gently and fold it tightly into a round loaf. Once shaped, place it directly into your floured proofing basket. 

---

### 4. Fermentation & Baking
* **Fermentation:** Let the shaped dough in its basket rest until about twice its size. Then transfer it directly to the fridge for an overnight cold proof (8–12 hours). This allows the flavor to slow-develop and gives it that signature sourdough tang.
* **Baking:** Preheat your oven to $460^\circ\text{F}$ ($240^\circ\text{C}$). Score the cold dough, transfer it to the hot oven, and bake for 45 minutes. In order to get a better crust, put an oven-resistant bowl with water (about 1 cup) into the oven together with the bread to create steam.
* **Resting:** Once baked, let the bread cool completely on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before slicing. If sliced while still hot, the bread will lose a lot of moisture and won't stay fresh for as long.
